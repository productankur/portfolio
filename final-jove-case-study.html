The Brief

Problem Statement

JoVE is renowned for its extensive library of research videos that assist academic researchers in understanding experimental techniques. The challenge was to tailor these resources to meet the specific needs of scientists in BioPharma companies — while leveraging existing content and being open to building entirely new products for this sector.

The core tension: JoVE's current platform serves universities and research groups well, but BioPharma scientists operate with different urgency, different resource constraints, and in many cases, in geographies with limited access to existing networks.

🎯

Core Objective

Expand JoVE's platform to serve BioPharma scientists in developing markets with content and tools that translate directly to lab outcomes.

Target Segments

BioPharma Start-Ups & MSMEs · Government-Aided Pharma Organisations · Private Label Pharmaceutical Manufacturers

Key Geographies

Sri Lanka · Bhutan · Nepal · Bangladesh · African continent (Kenya, Nigeria and beyond)

Shared Pain Points

Where the system fails them

01

Unable to replicate crucial experiments using text-only protocols — written instructions leave too much room for interpretation in a wet lab setting.

02

Unclear written protocols make reproduction expensive, time-consuming, and error-prone, burning through limited R&D budgets.

03

No structured access to industry experts — the knowledge held by experienced BioPharma scientists simply isn't reachable from emerging markets.

04

Reproducing experiments via on-site R&D or international travel is prohibitively expensive and slow — a journey that can take months and cost thousands.

Product Strategy

Two solutions

Rather than a single product, the strategy proposes two distinct offerings that serve different stages of the BioPharma journey — one for knowledge and capability-building, one for clinical execution.

1

JoVE for BioPharma — Enhanced Platform

Leverage JoVE's existing platform as an interface between academics, industry veterans, and small-to-medium BioPharma companies. The key insight: JoVE already has the content and the contributor network — the job is to surface it in a format that maps directly to lab outcomes, not academic publication.

End-to-end process videos Peer-reviewed content Expert access & mentorship LMS & Discussion Forum Live Webinars Encyclopedia AI Chatbot Data Simulators
2

JoVE Clinical Trial Platform

A new platform purpose-built for drug manufacturers in emerging markets to run structured clinical trials. Training data sourced from hospitals, diagnostics labs, and BioPharma corporates feeds an ML engine that produces real-time predictive reports — allowing companies like Hope Biotech to run trials that would otherwise require costly partnerships with established research CROs.

Patient Recruitment Clinical Trial Design Data Analysis Predictive Analytics Heart Attack Prediction Cervical Cancer Detection Covid-19 Chest Reports

High-Level Architecture

Solution 1 — Platform flow

Contributors from universities, private R&D labs, BioPharma corporates, and research groups submit content through JoVE's editorial pipeline (manuscript → peer review → filming → post-production). The platform then delivers this knowledge to consumers via LMS, forums, live webinars, encyclopedia, chatbot, and simulators.

Solution 1 — Content aggregation and distribution architecture from contributors to BioPharma consumers

Clinical Trial Architecture

Solution 2 — Data & ML pipeline

Clients (BioPharma start-ups, government organisations, private manufacturers) upload patient and trial data. This feeds a JoVE-hosted ML model trained on data from hospitals, diagnostics labs, and BioPharma corporates — producing real-time predictive reports that guide trial decisions.

Solution 2 — Clinical Trial Platform: data upload → ML training layer → predictive analysis → real-time reports

End-to-End Flow

From JoVE learner to clinical trial operator

The two solutions work in sequence: a company like Hope Biotech first builds capability on JoVE's learning platform, then — once they've secured government approvals and set up a manufacturing unit — migrates to the Clinical Trial Platform to run and analyse trials.

Discovery → Platform Demo

Hope Biotech team visits JoVE, attends a product demo. Chooses between standard courses or customised courses with mentorship.

Subscription → Learning access

Subscribes (team-based or monthly/yearly). Gets access to enrolled courses, discussion forums, case studies, success stories, and peer networking.

Successful trials → Manufacturing unit

After multiple successful lab trials and government approvals, sets up a manufacturing unit. Takes clinical trial approval.

Clinical Trial Platform — onboarding

Explores the Clinical Trial Platform, attends demo, opts for a 1-year subscription. Creates project, enters medicine details, connects with industry experts, receives clinical trial plan.

Patient recruitment → Trial execution

Downloads interested patient data and hospital/clinic information. Signs agreements. Creates subject profiles. Runs trial for a defined duration.

Data upload → ML analysis → Final report

Uploads post-trial data. ML model processes it. Outputs: Disease Progression Report, Treatment Effectiveness, Patient Health Score — enabling a go/no-go decision on the trial.
