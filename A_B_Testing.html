01 · The problem

Users were browsing — but not downloading

Cipla MedQuest is a pharma knowledge platform used by medical professionals, researchers, and clinicians to access peer-reviewed research papers. Despite strong traffic, the platform had a persistently low download conversion rate. Users were arriving, browsing papers — but not completing the one action the platform was built for.

The product team at Knimbus identified the download CTA button as a potential friction point. The button was present — but not visually prominent or compelling enough to drive action at the right moment in the user journey.

Core business problem

Low download conversion despite healthy session traffic and paper views

Suspected root cause

Weak CTA button — icon and placement not prominent enough at point of action

Users affected

Researchers, MDs, pharma professionals across India, UAE, Canada, France

02 · Hypothesis & approach

A focused test on one specific change

Rather than redesigning the full page, the team isolated the download CTA button — changing its icon and placement — as the single variable to test. This kept the experiment clean and the results attributable to one specific change.

"If we improve the icon and placement of the download CTA button, more users will complete a download in the same session."

Step 1 · Hypothesis

Define the change

New CTA button icon + placement vs existing design. One variable. Clean test.

Step 2 · Setup

Split the audience

5,000 users split 50/50. Cohort A got the old design. Cohort B got the new CTA.

Step 3 · Measure

Track 3 metrics

Download rate (primary) · Time on platform · Research papers viewed per session.

Test parameters
What changedCTA button icon and placement — Cohort B received the new version
Sample size5,000 users — Cohort A: 2,519 · Cohort B: 2,481
Duration2 weeks
LocationsIndia · UAE · Canada · France
DevicesDesktop · Mobile
Statistical methodChi-square test (conversion) · T-test (engagement) · 95% confidence threshold

05 · PM takeaways

What this exercise taught us

01

Small changes drive big results. A single button icon and placement change drove a 160% improvement in the platform's core metric. No full redesign needed.

02

The change was surgical, not broad. Time on platform and papers viewed barely moved — the new CTA specifically removed friction at the moment of action, without changing browsing behaviour.

03

Universal win — no segment left behind. The new design performed better on Desktop and Mobile, and across India, UAE, France, and Canada. A result that holds everywhere is a result you can trust.

04

Data beats assumptions. The team debated this change for weeks with opinions on both sides. The A/B test settled it in 2 weeks with 99% statistical confidence.
