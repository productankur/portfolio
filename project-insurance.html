Engineering Decisions

Key Design Choices

Intentional decisions that shape the model's accuracy and reliability.

01
Dual-Model Architecture Age ≤ 25 and Age > 25 populations have fundamentally different risk profiles. Separate models per segment capture these non-linear dynamics more accurately than a single model.
02
Segment-Specific Scalers Each model has its own fitted StandardScaler. Shared scalers would introduce distribution mismatch between training and inference for the young cohort.
03
Normalized Medical Risk Score Medical history is converted into a continuous normalized score (0–1) rather than raw categories, making it directly usable as a numeric feature without sparsity issues.
04
Ordinal Insurance Plan Encoding Bronze=1, Silver=2, Gold=3 preserves the natural ordering of plan tiers, which carries real premium signal and outperforms one-hot encoding for this feature.
05
Baseline Category Dropping One-hot encoded columns use the baseline-drop convention (e.g., Female, Northeast, Married are implicit baselines), preventing multicollinearity in the feature matrix.
06
Zero-Init Feature DataFrame The preprocessor initializes all 18 expected columns to zero before populating values. This guarantees the correct feature schema regardless of which options the user selects.

Medical Risk Scoring

Medical history is translated into a single normalized continuous score using domain-informed risk weights, then scaled to [0, 1] with a max possible score of 14.
